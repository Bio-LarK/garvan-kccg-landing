Clinician Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

Who can order the [G4 Pathology] clinical whole genome sequencing test?

The [G4 Pathology] clinical WGS test can only be ordered by a clinical geneticist or by specialist physicians supported by clinical geneticists or genetic counsellors.

At the time of ordering, the requesting practitioner should provide evidence of adequate genomic consent facilitated by a specialist genetic physician or genetic counsellor who have relevant experience, background and expertise

How to complete the [G4 Pathology] WGS request form

A separate form is required for each referral. If additional WGS requests are required, the first page of the electronic request form will appear again after clicking "Finish" with common fields automatically populated.

The online request form is divided into four pages to obtain information regarding

  • patient demographics
  • referring practitioner details
  • clinical information and
  • request, specimen and billing information.

Mandatory fields are underlined in red and must be completed before progressing to the next page of the form.

Detailed guidance on how to complete the [G4 Pathology] WGS screen request form can be found here. [PDF download]/open in a new window https://www.dropbox.com/s/5by2hvv4i0kffql/Guidance%20for%20Request%20Form_v1.docx?dl=0 ]

The referring practitioner's online/paper request form is retained for 7 years (adults) and until age 25 (>18 years old) in accordance with the requirements of the Health Records and Information Privacy Act (HRIPA), section 25 and [include full name] HPR, regulation 10. This is longer than the National Pathology Accreditation Advisory Council (NPAAC) minimum requirements.

What are the specimen collection and transport requirements?

For the [G4 Pathology] clinical WGS test, we accept only EDTA blood (minimum of 5mL for adult and 2 mL for paediatric specimens) or DNA (minimum of 100μL at a concentration of 10-100ng/μL).

The online request form should be printed and provided to the patient to have EDTA blood collected or sent to a laboratory where DNA is already stored to facilitate sending of the sample to:

Patient samples must be shipped at room temperature or with an ice pack but must not be frozen.

Blood samples should arrive at SydPath Central Specimen Reception within 3 days after collection. Sample transportation requirements are in line with NPAAC Requirements for Packaging and Transport of Path specimens and associated materials.

What information will the final report contain?

The [G4 Pathology] clinical WGS report will contain information on clinically significant or potentially significant variants. The report will also contain clinically relevant incidental findings. However, patients will be able to discuss this further with their referring clinician during the consenting process and choose whether they would like to be informed of these findings or not.

This is a diagnostic request form and so only variants with established disease associations will be reported. For children, genes causing purely adult onset diseases without preventative management in childhood will be unanalysed as far as is feasible with current knowledge. Analysis should be limited to gene panels extracted from WGS or paediatric onset disorders only.

In addition, adults may request a gene panel but may not have adult onset disorders excluded from analysis. Carrier status for autosomal recessive conditions may be undertaken but will only be confirmed and reported for adults or after consultation with the referring doctor in older teenagers who have reached sufficient maturity to undergo independent counselling and consent themselves (usually over the age of 16 years).

How will it be delivered?

The ordering clinician will be notified by SMS message when the report is complete. At this time, the report can also be retrieved electronically and will also delivered by either regular post and/or fax (as specified at time of ordering).

Does [G4 Pathology] offer re-analysis of samples?

Genomics is a rapidly evolving field where our understanding about which genes are implicated in a range of genetic disorders is constantly expanding. It is possible for the requesting geneticist to re-analyse WGS data as new genes are identified or if the clinical situation has changed. If you are considering this, please contact [G4 pathology] on (02) 9359 8002.

What does the test cost for a private patient?

The [G4 Pathology] clinical WGS test is $4,990 (excl. GST) for the proband and $3,390 (excl. GST) for each additional family member. [Julie to confirm whether GST applies]. This cost is subject to change. Please visit http://genomicpathology.garvan.org.au to confirm.

Private patients are required to complete a financial consent form and pay the full cost of the [G4 Pathology] clinical WGS before testing can commence. An invoice will be mailed to the email address nominated on the financial consent form.

We accept payment by BPAY only. Once payment is received we will email you a receipt, which has been coordinated with our specimen management system and commence WGS.

What sequencing platform does [G4 pathology] use for its test?

The [G4 Pathology] Whole Genome Sequencing is conducted using the Illumina HiSeq X Ten platform.

For more detailed information about the sequencing platform, the bioinformatics pipeline and its performance across certain areas of the genome or indeed individual genes please contact [G4 pathology] on (02) 9359 8002.
