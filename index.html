The Kinghorn Centre for Clinical Genomics

The Kinghorn Centre for Clinical Genomics is Australia’s only provider of whole genome sequences for clinical use. Whole genome sequencing is the most powerful approach available to diagnose genetic disease.

Clinical Whole Genome Sequencing Screen

The Clinical WGS Screen is a clinician-referred service that is intended for diagnosis of rare and heritable diseases. The Clinical WGS Screen is typically ordered for patients with a condition that has strong evidence for a genetic basis. The Clinical WGS Screen can be ordered for only the affected individual (Proband) or for the affected individual and both parents (Trio).

The genomes of the proband and parents (as applicable) are interpreted by an expert clinical team and all results are reviewed by a genetic pathologist. Samples may be provided as purified DNA or as blood. Further information on Clinical Whole Genome Sequencing is available for patients [subpage] and providers [subpage].

Research Whole Genome Sequencing Service

The Research Whole Genome Sequencing Service is a researcher-initiated service that is intended for ethically-approved human genome research studies. Samples sequenced through this service would typically be derived from research participants that had undergone informed consent for whole genome sequencing as part of an HREC-approved ethically-defensible research plan.

Please refer to our Research Whole Genome Sequencing Service website for further information.
