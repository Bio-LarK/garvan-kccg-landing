Clinical Whole Genome Sequencing

Clinical Whole Genome Sequencing (WGS) is a clinician-referred service that is intended for diagnosis of rare and heritable diseases. Clinical WGS is typically ordered for patients with a condition that has strong evidence for a genetic basis. Clinical WGS may be requested for only the affected individual (proband), for the affected individual and both parents (trio) or other affected family members. Genomes are interpreted by an expert clinical team and all results are reviewed by a genetic pathologist. Samples may be provided as purified DNA or as blood. Further information on clinical whole genome sequencing is available for patients and providers.

Research Whole Genome Sequencing Service

The Research Whole Genome Sequencing Service is a researcher-initiated service that is intended for human genome research studies that have been approved by a human research ethics committee (HREC). Samples sequenced through this service would be from research participants who have provided informed consent for WGS as part of an HREC-approved study.
